About

From architecture
to tattooing

Before

I studied architecture. I drew thousands of sketches. I spent five years in UX/UI design.

Then I understood one thing: the best design isn't where you add more — it's where you remove exactly what doesn't need to be there.

Now

I carried that thinking into tattooing.

I'm not trying to create something revolutionary or unlike anything else. My strength is in clean lines and composition — in being able to listen to your story and filter it through my visual language, without needless ego.

Every piece has to work on the body. Anatomically, compositionally, as a whole.

How I work

Three things I

No copying

I don't copy tattoos off the internet. Every project is made for one specific person — their story, their anatomy. One motif, once.

FAQ

Frequently
asked questions

Safety & hygiene

Total transparency. I open single-use tools in front of you, use barrier protection on the machine and work surfaces, and disinfect the furniture with medical-grade products after every session. Everything follows the current Czech hygiene standards.

Only certified premium-quality pigments approved in the EU. Since 2022, strict REACH rules apply across Europe (including Czechia) — I use only inks that fully comply, don't cause allergies and aren't banned anywhere.

Yes, my work is fully legal. I hold official accreditation for tattooing in the Czech Republic — the Cosmetic services and Tattooist (69-034-M) certificates. I'm not a "kitchen-table artist"; I work to all applicable regulations and professional standards.

Design & style

Of course. References are a great basis for understanding your taste. But I'll adapt someone else's finished design to my style and your anatomy — I don't copy other artists' work. The result will be unique and will work on your body.

I don't draw the design as a flat image but as an architectural object — accounting for muscle anatomy and the curves of your body. That's why the tattoo looks perfect both still and in motion.

We refine it for as long as it takes until you say "this is perfect." Small tweaks I make on the spot. I care about perfectionism in the details — I don't start tattooing until we're both happy.

Session & healing

A few simple steps to make the session comfortable:

  • A week before, moisturise the area regularly — well-hydrated skin takes pigment better.
  • On the day, don't apply anything — the skin must be clean and dry.
  • Get a good night's sleep and eat well before you come.
  • Skip alcohol for 24 hours beforehand — it affects blood thinning and lymph.
  • Wear comfortable clothes that give easy access to the tattooed area.

Pain is subjective. Fine lines are much easier and quicker to handle than dense filling. I don't recommend primary anaesthetic (creams) — it changes the skin's structure and worsens healing. For more sensitive areas we can apply a gentle secondary anaesthetic during the session.

I use a special healing film (Suprasorb F / equivalent), so for the first days you don't have to apply anything. You'll get full aftercare instructions in person and as a printed keepsake.
