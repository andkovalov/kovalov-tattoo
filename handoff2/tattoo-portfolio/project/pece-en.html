After the session

Tattoo aftercare

Good healing is half the result. Follow this guide — it keeps the lines clean, the colour rich and the skin calm. If you're ever unsure, message me and I'll gladly help.

Process for the Suprasorb F healing film and equivalents

01

Healing film right after the session

I'll cover the tattoo with a special healing film (Suprasorb F or equivalent). It works like a second skin — protecting the fresh tattoo from bacteria, so for the first days you don't have to apply anything or worry.

Lymph, blood and excess ink build up under the film — that's normal, don't be alarmed by the cloudy contents.

02

Removing the film

Leave the film on for 3–5 days (I'll tell you the exact time based on the motif and area). Remove it slowly under running lukewarm water — the water loosens the adhesive so it comes off comfortably.

Peel the film in the direction of hair growth, not straight off the skin. Never rip it off quickly when dry.

03

Washing and moisturising

After removing the film, care for the tattoo like this:

  • Wash the tattoo with lukewarm water and a mild, fragrance-free antibacterial soap. Use clean hands, not a washcloth.
  • Pat dry gently with a clean paper towel — never rub.
  • Apply a thin layer of healing cream 2–3 times a day (Panthenol, Bepanthen or equivalent). The cream should soften the skin, not "drown" it.

Keep moisturising through healing, roughly 10–14 days, until the skin no longer feels tight.

04

What to avoid during healing

For the first ~14 days, better skip:

  • Baths, pools, saunas and the sea — no long soaking.
  • Direct sun and tanning beds. Once healed, always protect the tattoo with SPF 50.
  • Scratching or picking scabs and flakes — even if it itches. You'll be left with scars and patchy colour.
  • Intense sport and sweating for the first few days.
  • Tight clothing that rubs against the tattoo.
05

How healing goes

Every skin heals a little differently, but it usually looks like this:

Days 1–5 Film

The tattoo is under the healing film. You don't apply anything — just check the film holds and doesn't leak.

Days 5–14 Peeling

The skin starts to peel and itch, fine flakes may form. Wash regularly and moisturise thinly.

Weeks 3–6 Settling

The surface has healed, deeper skin layers mature. The colour "settles" and stabilises to its final look.

06

When to contact me

Mild redness, swelling and warmth in the first days are normal. But message me if you notice:

  • stronger pain, swelling or redness that worsens after a few days,
  • pus, an unusual smell or fever,
  • any uncertainty about healing — better ask right away.

Minor touch-ups after healing are handled individually. Your satisfaction with the result is my priority.
