About me

From architecture
to tattooing

Before

I studied architecture. Drew thousands of sketches. Spent five years in UX/UI design.

Then I realized one thing: the best design isn't where you add more — it's where you remove exactly what shouldn't be there.

Now

I brought that thinking into tattooing.

I don't aim to create something revolutionary or unlike anything else. My strength lies in the clarity of lines and composition. In listening to your story and channelling it through my visual language — without unnecessary ego.

Every piece must work on the body. Anatomically, compositionally, as a whole.

My approach

Three things I never
compromise on

01
No copying

I don't replicate tattoos from the internet. Every project is created for a specific person — their story, their anatomy. One motif — once.

02
Anatomical composition

I build the sketch accounting for body dynamics. In motion, the tattoo doesn't distort — on the contrary, it accentuates muscle form and curves.

03
No-compromise sterility

Premium pigments EU-certified to REACH standards, and single-use materials I open exclusively in your presence.

FAQ

Common
questions

Safety & hygiene

Full transparency. I open single-use instruments in front of you, use barrier protection on the machine and work surfaces, and disinfect furniture with medical-grade products after every session — strictly following Czech hygiene regulations.

Only premium, EU-certified pigments. Since 2022 Europe has enforced strict REACH regulations — I use only compliant inks that are hypoallergenic and unrestricted anywhere in the EU.

Yes, my practice is fully legal. I hold official Czech accreditation for tattoo work — certificates for Cosmetic Services and Tattoo Artist (69-034-M). I'm not a home studio: I operate under all current regulations and professional standards.

Design & style

Absolutely. References are a great starting point to understand your taste. But I adapt any existing design to my style and your anatomy — I don't copy other artists' work. The result will be unique and work correctly on your body.

I draw the sketch as an architectural object, not a flat image — accounting for muscle anatomy and the curves of your body. This means the tattoo looks perfect both at rest and in motion.

We refine it as many times as needed until you say "that's exactly it." I won't start tattooing until we're both satisfied.

Session & healing

A few simple steps:

  • A week before: moisturise the area regularly — well-hydrated skin takes pigment better.
  • On the day: don't apply anything — skin should be clean and dry.
  • Get enough sleep and eat a good meal beforehand.
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours — it affects blood coagulation.
  • Wear comfortable clothing with easy access to the tattoo area.

Pain is subjective. Fine line work is noticeably easier and faster than solid fill. I don't recommend topical numbing creams — they alter skin structure and compromise healing. On sensitive areas, mild secondary anaesthesia can be applied during the session.

I use a specialist healing film (Suprasorb F or equivalent), so for the first few days you don't need to apply anything. Full aftercare instructions are given in person and in print.

Everything is covered in detail on → Tattoo aftercare.
